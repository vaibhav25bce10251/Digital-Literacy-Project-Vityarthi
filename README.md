## Project Overview

This repository contains my Digital Literacy Project submitted for the course CSE0001 at VIT Bhopal University. The project is based on the role of a Student Digital Ambassador and includes work related to digital awareness, student portfolio building, coding and collaboration tools, professional communication, and cybercrime awareness.

The purpose of this project is to understand how students can use digital platforms and online tools in a safe, responsible, and professional manner. Each task in this repository represents one important area of digital literacy and shows practical learning through activities, screenshots, written reflections, and awareness material.

This task helped me understand that building a digital portfolio is important from the first year itself. These platforms can be improved over time and can become useful for projects, internships, skill development, and future academic and career opportunities.

## Task 4 – Draft a Professional Email and Etiquette Guide

For Task 4, I wrote two professional emails and prepared a checklist on responsible social media use for college students.

The first email is a formal request to a professor asking for an extension on an assignment submission deadline. The second email is addressed to an internship coordinator expressing interest in a summer internship opportunity. While writing both emails, I focused on clear subject lines, proper greetings, formal tone, and respectful sign-offs.

I also created a Social Media Do’s and Don’ts checklist to show how students should behave responsibly on online platforms. This checklist includes simple practices such as verifying information before sharing, maintaining respectful communication, protecting privacy, and avoiding harmful or misleading content.

This task helped me understand that professional communication is an important part of digital literacy. The way a student writes emails and uses social media can strongly affect their academic image and professional reputation.

## Task 5 – Cybercrime Awareness Case Study and Prevention Guide

For Task 5, I selected the topic of UPI and online payment fraud. This form of cybercrime is highly relevant for students because digital payment apps are now used regularly for daily transactions such as food orders, travel payments, shopping, and money transfers.

In this task, I prepared a case study explaining what UPI fraud is, how it usually takes place, who is commonly targeted, and what kind of impact it can have on victims. I focused on a realistic student-related scenario to make the case study more practical and relatable.

I also created a Stay Safe Online checklist with preventive steps that college students can follow in their everyday digital life. The checklist includes both general cyber safety measures and UPI-specific precautions. It also mentions the official reporting channels: National Cyber Crime Portal and Helpline 1930.

This task helped me understand that cybercrime often succeeds not because technology is weak, but because people act in panic, trust fake messages, or fail to verify information. It made me more aware of the importance of alertness and responsible digital behaviour.
